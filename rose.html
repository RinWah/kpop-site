rose

i never got far into rose's career until her 'gone' album came out. since then, i've followed her through her career as she released 'rosie'. ever since, i can't imagine her going back into a group setting. blackpink definitely caught me off guard with their combo to release 'jump'.

trainee era

rose started her career with YG in 2012, placing amongst one of the top 700 who auditioned. she debuted in 2016 with blackpink after only four years of training. she was one of the members with the least amount of training and was often bullied about being inexperienced.

blackpink era

on aug 8, 2016, rose debuted with blackpink as a main vocalist. they released many popular songs tgt as a group, such as 'how you like that', 'kill this love', and 'pink venom'.

solo era

rose had started her solo career with 'gone' and then after the group took a more formal hiatus, released her whole rosie album, which she collaborated with Bruno Mars to release APT. the song went viral and she gained more traction on her other songs and was more recognized for her vocal talent, which is unfortunately more uncommon with the rise of visuals in the k-pop world.
